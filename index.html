01

The Mark

Three-peak mountain silhouette — built on a grid, works at any size.

Three peaks. One identity.

Three overlapping peaks — one for each pillar: Strategy, Margins, People. The center peak is tallest, grounding the composition. Read it as a mountain range, read it as growth — it works either way.

  • ConstructionGrid-aligned; all angles 45° or 90°
  • Min size16px wide on screen · 8mm in print
  • Clear space½ × peak-height on every side, always
  • FillSolid only — no outlines, no gradients, no textures

05

Usage Rules

Keep it consistent. When in doubt, keep it simple.
✔

Use on approved backgrounds

Ink mark on Paper/Cream. Cream mark on Ink/Pine. These are the only two modes.

✔

Respect clear space

Always maintain ½ × peak-height of clear space around the mark. No text, borders, or graphics inside this zone.

✔

Use approved lockups

Pick from the six lockups in §02. Never rearrange the mark, wordmark, or tagline into a new composition.

✔

Solid fill only

The mark is always a single solid color. No gradients, no outlines, no drop shadows, no textures.

✘

Don't stretch or rotate

The mark must maintain its original proportions. Never squash, stretch, skew, or rotate it.

✘

Don't add effects

No drop shadows, bevels, glows, emboss, or any other effect. The mark speaks for itself.

✘

Don't use unapproved colors

The mark should only appear in Ink (#1A1815) or Cream (#F1ECE2). Never in Ochre, Rust, or arbitrary colors.

✘

Don't go below minimum size

16px on screen, 8mm in print. Below this, the three peaks become indistinct.
